Kek batik is a Malaysian refrigerator cake delicacy influenced by tiffin, which was brought in by the British during the colonial era and altered with Malaysian ingredients. Broken Marie biscuits are combined with a chocolate sauce or a runny custard made with egg, butter/margarine, condensed milk, Milo, and chocolate powders to make this cake. The cake is served on exceptional occasions such as Eid al-Fitr or birthday celebrations.

A Marie biscuit resembles a rich tea cookie in appearance. Mara is another name for it. The name is normally embossed on the top surface of the cookie, and the borders are also embossed with an ornate design. It's made with wheat flour, sugar, palm or sunflower seed oil, and vanilla flavouring, unlike the rich tea biscuit.

Cocoa butter, the fatty component of the bean, is extracted from chocolate liquor, roasted cocoa beans that have been ground into a liquid state. Dry cocoa solids are the components of cocoa beans that remain after cocoa butter, the fatty component of the bean, is extracted from chocolate liquor. Cocoa butter accounts for 46 to 57 percent of the weight of cocoa beans and is responsible for the melting qualities of chocolate. The powdered form of the dry solids with a minor amount of cocoa butter is called cocoa powder.
